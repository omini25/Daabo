Is device insurance for my phone worth it?

Yes. Phones are fragile, expensive, and easy to damage.

What is cell device insurance?

Device insurance plans protect against damage to your device that isn't covered by the manufacturer's warranty, in addition to protecting against theft and Loss. It's like any other insurance plan where you pay a monthly or yearly premium for your coverage, and then your device is protected in the event of a covered loss.

Get Started

How does device insurance work?

When purchasing device insurance, choose the coverage amount and policy term that fits your needs. Coverage amounts will vary and policy terms last anywhere from one month to one year. In the event of a covered loss, we will pay to repair or replace your phone, minus your deductible.

Liquid damage

Accidental device damage

Accidental damage

What isn't covered by device insurance?

Here's a list of what a device insurance plan typically won't cover:

Tampering

Modifications

Natural wear and tear

Cosmetic flaws

Unauthorized repairs

Device Accessories

Your device will come with a manufacturer's warranty that usually covers any defects or malfunctions that are the fault of the manufacturer. For example, if your charging port, camera, or software is defective and it's a manufacturing error, your device warranty would cover the cost of fixing or replacing your device.

How much is a device insurance plan?

The cost of your device insurance plan can vary based on the type of device, and the amount of your deductible. Why do I need device insurance?

The high cost to repair or replace a phone can make phone insurance a near necessity. And for most people, their phone is a vital part of their day-to-day life, whether it's for business or personal use. Suddenly damaging your phone immediately disrupts your ability to communicate with friends, family, and co-workers. But with smartphone insurance, a potentially expensive and inconvenient problem becomes very easy to fix. Still not sure if you need mobile phone protection? Consider these questions when deciding if you should get cell phone insurance:

Do you use your cell phone to stay connected with family and friends?

Do you use your phone for work, emergencies, internet access, or all of the above?

Do you have a history of having your phone stolen or dropping it?

Do you use your phone while exercising or being active?

Do you have children who use your phone?

Do you own an expensive smartphone or iPhone that would be costly to replace without insurance?

Do you rely on your personal cell phone for business and would need a repair or replacement as soon as possible if it was damaged or stolen?

Do you work in extreme conditions near water or heavy machinery that could put your phone at risk?

If your answer is "yes" for any of the above, then cell phone insurance can be a smart choice. If you're not prepared to spend hundreds of dollars to repair or replace your phone at any moment, cell phone insurance can save you time, money, and the headache of being stranded with a broken phone.
